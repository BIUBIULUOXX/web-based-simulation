My Topic

We research three major parts

Nowadays, diabetes is posing a threat to a lot of people. This project aims to design a process to figure out the influence of processing factors like hypertension, age, BMI, gender on (type 2) diabetes and to illustrate the visualization of diabetes (type 2) data with some related factors. The specific reasons for the impact of these factors have not yet to be examined. Specifically, it is related to problems about whether hypertension associated with age, BMI and gender matters diabetes or not and how big the impact (He, J. et al 2000 p.546). Given that hypertension is a common and longterm medical condition and it is also a major risk for any disease. In this case, we assume that hypertension has a significant effect on diabetes (type 2). There are other issues that we can study in-depth, such as how to classify the people who may have high risks in diabetes.

Hypertension

Given that hypertension is a common and longterm medical condition and it is also a major risk for any disease. In this case, we assume that hypertension has a significant effect on diabetes (type 2).

Age

Some researchers believe that the risk of diabetes in younger age groups is lower than in older groups (Krakoff 2003 p.80). This is strong proof that the onset age can make a difference to T2DM

BMI

The results also suggested that BMI(body mass index) was an essential parameter in hypertension(Nur, N. et al 2008 p.88). Thus, we should take BMI into account for the development of hypertension.

Results

main results shown below

From the first figure below, we can see that there are many influence factors. We chose some of these factors (Age BMI Blood Pressure) so as to simply the project.

The impact of increasing levels of BMI on age at onset on T2DM is seen augmented in patients diagnosed with hypertension.

Upon considering BMI as a categorical variable, we find that while the mean onset age of T2DM in hypertensive patients decreases by as much as 5 - 12 years in every higher BMI categories, significant decrease in non-hypertensive patients exists only when severely obese.

Hazard due to hypertension (against the baseline of non-hypertension and normal weight) increases at least two-fold in every obese category. While males have higher hazard due to hypertension in early adulthood, females have higher hazard in late adulthood.

Discussion

findings, strengths, limitations

Findings: It is clearly shown that the pre-existing condition of hypertension augments the association of BMI with Type 2 diabetes onset in both males and females.
Strengths: The presented results provide health professionals directives on the extent of weight-loss required to delay onset of Type 2 diabetes in hypertensive versus non-hypertensive patients.
Limitation: We only chose 3 main factors(age BMI hypertension). In the future, we can select more variables to build the model.
